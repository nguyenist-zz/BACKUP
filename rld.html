Your walk is: long

Project Background

This project was inspired by an interesting problem I found while conducting usability studies for Landmark. Many commuters, city dwellers and tourists use navigation apps such as Google Maps to get them from point A to point B. These apps tell pedestrians to "Walk 0.3 Miles and turn Left on Broadway" but very few people knew how to visualize that distance on the go.

I built this tool to help users understand how long 300 feet actually is using the lengths of fun objects, humans and animals. If you visually know the length of a metro car, you might understand that 400 feet means about 5-6 metro cars. An interesting fact: people who played sports that required the constant measurement of distances baseball, track, etc. usually found it easier to understand distances while navigating.
