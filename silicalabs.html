SILICA LABS

Designing and developing apps for small screen devices.

Silica Labs Software Corp, our Washington, D.C. based startup is recognized as a global leader in software development, particularly for mobile and wearable software. Together since April 2013, we have created software for some of the most innovative companies in the world, including Mapbox, SingTel, Social Radar, Surescripts. We also created a proprietary platform used by media brands, including National Geographic, and the Atlantic, to distribute their content through mobile and wearable devices. These companies are very different, ranging from a social-location startup to a 100 year- old nonprofit, a billion-dollar software company to a telecommunications giant larger than AT&T. Moreover, each software application met different technical and business objectives.

Mapbox: Offline Maps for Android Wear

Mapbox is one of the biggest providers of custom online maps for major websites such as Four-square, Pinterest, Evernote, the Financial Times and Uber Technologies. We worked with the development team to build Mapbox’s Android SDK for Android Wear. We prepared instructions on how to build an Android Wear app using Mapbox's SDK and designed and built a demo app Mapbox SDK and custom offline maps. This concepts shows the possibility of building beautiful, offline maps to smartwatches such as the Moto360, Samsung Galaxy Gear and the Samsung Smartwatch.

Social Radar: For Google Glass

Social Radar is a startup that merges identity and location, enabling people to know when friends are nearby. Its CEO, Michael Chasen, previously was CEO and founder of Blackboard, the education company he recently sold for $1.7 billion. Social Radar hired us to help their team architect their product on wearable devices, mainly Google Glass. We worked closely with their team to design and develop a Glass app that displays nearby friends, identifies rele-vant people in the room based on location and identify, and activates a “radar” enabling the user to follow an arrow displayed in Glass towards the nearby friend. We integrated with their databases and backend technology regarding users’ identities, location, privacy preferences, and interests.

National Geographic: YourShot




National Geographic is a nonprofit headquartered in Washington, DC and founded in 1888. In 2013 it sought (1) an app on Google Glass with a feature set matching those of the first released media apps by New York Times, CNN, and Elle. It later sought a software integration (2) to send photos directly from a Glass device to the company’s photo blog, so that National Geographic photographers in the field could directly share first hand photos from the device with all of the photo blog’s readers. We worked with them to design and build both applications. We had to integrate with their content databases both to access and input images, text, and video. It enabled any media company to create an app and send media content to Google Glass using a simple dashboard and without writing a line of code. This software was featured in Fast Company and Mashable.

Marina Bay Sands Hotel Singapore

The Marina Bay Sands Hotel is an luxury resort facing the Marina Bay in Singapore. Developed by Las Vegas Sands, it is billed as the world's most expensive standalone casino property at $8 billion, including cost of the prime land. Visiting the rooftop is one of its most popular attractions, allowing viewers to see a breathtaking view of the skyline and landmarks. Attracted by the latest developments of heads-up display technology, the team wanted to prototype what it might look like to explore the views of Marina Bay through Google Glass. We prototyped the design concepts which used the compass to pinpoint landmarks, audio enhancements and photo taking.
